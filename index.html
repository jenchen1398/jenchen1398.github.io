About

I am an undergraduate studying computer science and music at Columbia and a dedicated violinist of over 10 years.

I love finding ways in which technology itself can be stretched to new limits when integrated with other fields. In high school, I interned at the San Diego Supercomputer Center, where I implemented spatial sound in a Raspberry Pi parallel programming cluster and discovered that technology can immerse audiences in a surround sound experience. At Columbia, I'm applying skills I've learned in honors computer science courses to build apps that benefit the greater Columbia & NYC community. As a member of ADI Labs, my team and I built a course recommendation web app (CU Course Mapper) for computer science students at Columbia and are expanding it to a wider group of students. Through participating in hackathons, I hope to create more projects, like the social activism platform (NYCAct) my team and I built at Columbia Devfest, & meet more of the wider CS community.

As a violinist, I've studied with top professors at USC, Pepperdine, & Juilliard, and have earned many awards in local & international competitions. I've been invited to perform at Carnegie Hall as a winner of the American Protégé International Concerto Competition and as a trio in the Columbia Chamber Players, and have served as co-concertmaster of the Idyllwild Arts Orchestra. Most recently, I attended the top classical music festival in the U.S., Aspen Music Festival, and was a violinist in the Aspen Conducting Academy. I'm passionate about spreading music to a wide audience and making education and music accessible and empowering to youth. During high school, I founded the Music Outreach Club and led a team of 30 people to provide free music education & music therapy to youth and seniors, and hosted benefit concerts to raise thousands of dollars for charities such as Hands 4 Honduras & Liberty in North Korea. I currently play in the Columbia University Orchestra 1st violin section, Columbia Music Performance Program, and study with Prof. Joel Smirnoff at Juilliard on scholarship from the Columbia Dolan Prize.
